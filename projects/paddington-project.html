About This Project

Bringing a Victorian masterpiece back to life

This Paddington home featured a stunning Victorian-era decorative plaster ceiling — an intricate grid of raised relief panels filled with elaborate floral and foliage motifs, framed by ornate period cornicing. Years of movement and water damage had left a significant hole punched through one of the central panels, disrupting what is a genuinely remarkable piece of craftsmanship.

Restoring decorative relief work of this complexity demands patience and a deep familiarity with traditional plastering techniques. The damaged section required careful removal and build-up, matching the surrounding relief profile by hand. Every leaf, petal, and border had to flow seamlessly into the existing moulding — the kind of work that becomes invisible when done well.

The finished ceiling now looks exactly as it was intended — a cohesive, richly detailed surface that sets the tone for the whole room. The restored plasterwork sits beneath a crystal chandelier that shows off every detail of the relief work, a combination that makes the result particularly rewarding to see.

Scope of Work

  • check_circle Careful removal of damaged plaster section without disturbing surrounding relief panels
  • check_circle Hand-matched restoration of Victorian floral relief motifs to original profile
  • check_circle Seamless integration with existing grid panels and framing mouldings
