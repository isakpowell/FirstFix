About This Project

Bringing an ornate ceiling back to life

This residential property in Dulwich featured a striking decorative plaster ceiling — a Victorian-era design with repeating ornate relief panels, intricate scrollwork and botanical motifs set within a grid of moulded borders. A section of one panel had sustained significant damage, leaving a large hole that cut through the fine detailing.

The challenge with work like this lies in the precise matching of relief depth, texture, and profile. Ornate decorative plaster panels each have subtle variations in the way motifs are laid out, and any repair that doesn't account for this reads as obviously patched. Our approach was to carefully rebuild the damaged area in layers, using traditional plaster techniques to recreate the original relief by hand.

The result is a seamless restoration — the repaired section sits flush with the surrounding panels, with the pattern flowing continuously across the repair boundary as though the damage never occurred. The finished ceiling is ready to paint, with no visible trace of the original hole.

Scope of Work

  • check_circle Assessment of damage extent and pattern matching across surrounding panels
  • check_circle Structural backing and keying of the hole to accept new plaster
  • check_circle Layered plaster build-up to match panel depth and surrounding profile
  • check_circle Hand-tooled relief work to recreate the original Victorian decorative pattern
  • check_circle Final finish sanded flush and left paint-ready
